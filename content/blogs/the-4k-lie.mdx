## Introduction

Most people think video quality is determined solely by resolution.

1080p contains roughly 2 million pixels, while 4K contains over 8 million pixels. On paper, 4K should look four times better. However, pixels alone don't determine image quality. The amount of information stored within those pixels matters just as much.

Imagine printing a huge poster from a low-quality image. The poster is larger, but the details aren't magically improved. The same principle applies to video.

Lets take a layman example  think quality as the size of the pizza.

- 1080p = Medium Pizza
- 4K = Large Pizza

A larger pizza gives you more surface area, but that doesn't automatically make it taste better.

Similarly, 4K gives you more pixels, but those pixels still need quality ingredients.

## The Hidden Number Nobody Talks About: Bitrate

If resolution tells you how many pixels exist, bitrate tells you how much information those pixels contain.

> **Bitrate is the amount of data used every second to represent a video.**

A high bitrate means **better detail retention**, **cleaner motion**, **better shadow detail**, **less compression**.

A low bitrate means **blurry textures**, **color banding**, **blocking artifacts**, **smudged motion**.

This is where OTT platforms start to get interesting.
